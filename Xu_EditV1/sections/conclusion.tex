\section{Conclusion and Future Work}

This paper argues that the relevant design question in educational AI is not simply whether retrieval should be used, but when classical retrieval-centered pipelines are sufficient and when more explicit orchestration becomes justified. Rather than claiming universal superiority for any single architecture, the paper offers a task framing and an evaluation perspective for comparing classical RAG, agentic RAG, and multi-agent systems under different educational conditions. Future work will focus on empirically instantiating this framework, including preliminary comparisons across task regimes and more explicit evaluation of groundedness, pedagogical quality, adaptivity, and cost-latency trade-offs.
